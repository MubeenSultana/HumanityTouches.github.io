Our Mission

To elevate mental health awareness, reduce stigma, and expand culturally responsive support for Latino individuals who have undergone chemotherapy—through bilingual education, peer connection, and trusted community partnerships—so they and their families can heal and thrive.

Our Vision

A future where Latino cancer survivors and their families have equitable access to trauma-informed, culturally grounded mental health care; feel seen and supported in their language and culture; and experience resilient, dignified survivorship within strong, connected communities.

Community Outreach

At Humanity Touches, we mobilize the community to provide for unhoused members, support seniors, strengthen food security, provide inclusive teaching for people with disabilities, and offer free pickleball clinics for underserved families—rooted in bilingual, culturally responsive, and trauma-informed practices.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can participate?
Anyone in our service area — priority for seniors, unhoused neighbors, people with disabilities, and cancer survivors.
Is everything free?
Yes — all services, clinics, and programs are 100% free.
Do you offer mental health resources?
Yes — education, support groups, and referrals to culturally competent counseling.
Do I need pickleball experience?
No! Gear is provided and coaches teach beginners — all levels welcome.
